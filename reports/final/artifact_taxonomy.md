# Artifact Taxonomy

The PEARL SFT discovery campaign has identified several failure modes where language-model generation repeatedly exploits repeat/domain-duplication shortcuts to satisfy catalytic geometry constraints unless explicitly punished.

These failure modes are formally captured as named classes:

- **Class A**: Long tandem repeat artifact, 30aa+ (Observed in v2.3)
- **Class B**: Micro-repeat boundary artifact, 21aa (Observed in v2.4 surfing the 20aa gate)
- **Class C**: Shrinking-boundary artifact, 16aa (Observed in v2.5 surfing the 15aa gate)
- **Class D**: Topology-level repeat-dependent artifact, detected by 8aa masking (Observed in the original v2 Unicorn)
- **Class E**: Clean topology-independent bridge candidate (v2.5-Hit2, True Unicorn v1)
